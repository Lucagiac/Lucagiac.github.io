Robotic Arm Projects

In this first stage I started to look around for projects that can inspire my work, both from a technical and an interaction point ov view.

Technical Implementation

If the robotic arm, in this case a UR-10, will have to pick up different masks, it needs to have an automated mechanical system that does it. Battery vs power of the robotic arm.

Mechanical Solution

The first and simplest idea is to use a sort of a hook. That would be easily implementable but would be limiting in terms of movement.

Electromagnetic Solution

Using a powerful electromagnet that could be run from the internal power supply of the Ur-10 the system could pick up lightweight end-effectors. On the negative side, the magnets could interfere with the motors inside the robotic arm.

Hydraulic Solution

The third option would be to build a suction cup that can pick up each end-effector. Similarly to the electromagnetic solution, only lightweight pieces could be lifted.

Science Fiction Characters

A total of 5 inspiring non-humanoid robots were so far selected, both from literature and cinema.

Robot not named, Runaround (Isaac Asimov)

The first literature inspiration is a story from Asimov. Keywords: physical control, human-centered approach.

“Powel stared and then pinched his moustache. ‘Uh… Oh!’ Donovan’s eyes bulged. ‘We’ve got to ride him? Like a horse?’ ‘I guess that’s the idea. I don’t know why, though.’ […] He placed a foot in the improvised stirrup and swung upward. He found the seat comfortable; there was the humped back of the robot, evidently shaped for the purpose, a shallow groove along each shoulder for the thighs and two elongated ears whose purpose now seemed obvious. Powell seized the ears and twisted the head. His mount turned ponderously. ‘Lead on Macduff.’ But he did not feel at all light-hearted.” [Asimov, Isaac. I, Robot. London: Dobson, 1950. pp. 36-37.
