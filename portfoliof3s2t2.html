collaborative robot painter

Is it possible to bring back intuitive and expressive qualities in controllers of modern CNC machines? I decided to investigate this topic in the context of robotic art, which has long been investigated by artists and engineers. Hence, I outlined four separate functionalities for the robotic arm: an on/off switch, a speed modulator, a recorder and a manipulator.

The robotic arm could then be (almost) live controlled through the use of the interface, via Processing software. In fact, the robot follows a pre-set drawing trajectory. The user could then interact with the controllers and modify the path.

The final result is quite interesting - and poses relevant questions on the dialogue between the robot and the user.

In the end, who is the creative agent in this context? I believe that good interaction is about being a continuous dialogue.

what was behind the interface?

Interface design has long been inspired by science fiction movies, as well as novels written a couple dozen years ago really anticipated our contemporary technological context. One can think of Arthur Clarke or Isaac Asimov.

So I started to research virtuous examples in sci-fi novels and realised four interface prototypes.

In controller 1: the switch, the user can place his hand in the assigned slot and touch the capacitive sensors. When the hand is released,the robotic arm will switch off. Eventually, the robot can never be unattended. Similarly, in the story “Runaround” by Isaac Asimov, astronaut needs to physically climb and sit on top of the robot in order to switch it on.

Controller 2: the manipulator works pretty much like a joystick, allowing for a two-axis control and a third axis mastery through pressing. If the hand is released at any moment, the springs will cause the controller to move in a silly way. Similarly, in the movie Star Wars, the characters Drum D-Series are droids that possess a very springy head. When touched, it moves in a particular way.

Controller 3: the recorder is composed of two slots and multiple cassettes that can be inserted and removed. While the cassette is located in the upper slot, the device is recording the performed movements. Otherwise, when the cassette is place within the lower slot, the interface replays the movement. Similarly, in the movie Aliens, a phone interface with no buttons appears. In order ton place a call, the user simply puts a contact card in the interface.

In controller 4: the speedmeter, the linear sequence of blocks gives a constant visual feedback on which percentage of the total speed is being run. Removing one block corresponds to lowering the velocity, until when 0 blocks means a switch-off. Similarly, the artificial intelligence HAL 9000 also has a physical body in 2001, A Space Odyssey. The picture above showsits computational skills and memory in blocks.

That's how this interface study was carried out in London.
