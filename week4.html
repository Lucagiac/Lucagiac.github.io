Summary of the week
Most of the time this week ended up being used for preparing the portfolio and the story to tell at the final crit presentation. Nonetheless I managed to build a prototype of a puppet robotic arm.

Robotic Arm Puppet

Inspired by the puppetry readings of last week I decided to put together a puppet with three simple axis. It is composed of three sections, of which P1 one can rotate around its own axis. P2 is the part that can be puppetteered through the use of four fishing wires attached to the extremities. The puppet can pick up different end effectors through a magnetic mechanism. P3 consists of a soft sponge material interface, while P4 is a blob of metal staples.


The video below does show how this work and how this could be scaled up to the use of a real robotic arm.
