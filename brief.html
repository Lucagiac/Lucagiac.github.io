HRii: intuitive interfaces for robotic arm manipulation

Through an historic overview of sci-fi relevant literature, I will evaluate first and inspire later sets of controllers for an intuitive and expressive robotic arm manipulation.

Brief

Industrial CNC machines such as 3D-printers, laser-cutters and robotic arms have reached design and architecture studios for quite some years now. The more CAM (Computer Aided Manufacturing) tools populated our desks, the more professionals got confined to a making process which consists of preparing a computer file solely. The designer is totally detached from the actual making process, whereas in the old days he used to be in control of it through the tools he was handling. Is it possible to bring back intuitive and expressive qualities in controllers of modern times?

If that was the goal, now it comes the strategy. The 20th century saw humanities influencing the development of technological milestones and vice-versa. Science fiction novels and movies proved to be able to subtly shape the collective imaginary of development at large and human interaction with technical devices in particular. Can science fiction props and tools inspire a generation of controllers for CNC machines?

Keywords: Human-Robot Interaction, Intuitive and Aesthetic Interactiton, Speculative Design

Preliminary Studies on Interaction

#1 The first robot investigates the territory of pain and shared empathy. The underlying assumption is that if a robot can simulate pain, it does actually allow for mistreatment.
#2 The second prototype is a study on intimacy through touch.
#3 The third prototype focuses on joyful and silly behaviours. While initially the response of the robot is coherent, later it becomes very unpredictable in a playful way.
#4 The fourth prototype focuses on wild behaviour and a taming process. Without human intervention, the robot behaves with rapid and umpredictable moves. When the human is recognised by the sensor, the stick follows the hand quietly.



Moving further from the independent interaction studies towards the possible applications with a UR-10 robotic arm, I got inspired by puppetry. I built a reproduction of a robotic arm that could let me explore:
-movement through a set of poses of the robotic arm;
-interactivity with a set of end effectors.

A Consolle for Controlling a UR-10

In light of the studies presented above and inspired by science-fiction masterpieces, I built three parts of a controller for robotic arm. For now only the simulation work, but hopefully soon will be implemented with real robots!

Switching on

In order to switch the robot on one needs to perform this gesture of physical dominance and abandonment.

Operating

The robotic arm, whatever end effector is mounted on it, can be operated through this moving disc.

Switching off

In order to switch the robot off, it has to be physically disabled through the gesture of removing parts of it.

Recording and playing back

The tokens are like memory sticks that can be loaded with content and record movements while in the upper socket. In a similar way, content can be played back when the token is in the lower socket.
