Investigating the relationship between humans and robots

From self-manufactured robots to controllers for robotic arms to human-robot performance.

Last updated on September 26, 2017
“We are lonely but fearful of intimacy. Digital connection and sociable robots may offer the illusion of companionship without the demand of friendship” -Sherry Turkle-

Overview

These three research areas are not to be seen as intependent studies. On the contrary they all contributed to the final piece, which shows how the behaviour of the robot, together with the control from the actor and the qualities of the materials involved can foster a meaningful interaction.

Studies of Behaviour

I build four prototypes where I tried to subvert a paradigm. Instead of building “a robot that caresses you” where clearly the focus is about the interaction with it, I named them with their particular associated attitude.
#1 The first robot investigates the territory of pain and shared empathy. The underlying assumption is that if a robot can simulate pain, it does actually allow for mistreatment.
#2 The second prototype is a study on intimacy through touch.
#3 The third prototype focuses on joyful and silly behaviours. While initially the response of the robot is coherent, later it becomes very unpredictable in a playful way.
#4 The fourth prototype focuses on wild behaviour and a taming process. Without human intervention, the robot behaves with rapid and umpredictable moves. When the human is recognised by the sensor, the stick follows the hand quietly.



Studies of Control

The second part of my research involved designing and testing a set of direct controllers. Inspired by science-fiction characters, the interface allows for a direct manipulation of a UR10 robotic arm. If we separate the function of a robotic arm from its actual purpose, we can list a set of few basic functions that a robitic arm need to perform in order to achieve any task. These are:
#1 Moving the tool in space;
#2 Controlling the speed;
#3 Switching on/off;
#4 Recording and playing sequences of movements;



Studies of Aesthetic

The last part of my research investigated the role of aesthetics and material properties in designing human-robot systems. Going beyond paradigms of antromorphism, how does the aesthetics of robots inspire trust in its users?
