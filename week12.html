Week 11

Looking back at the process, attempt to make a synthesis

Posted on July 30, 2017

Summary of the week
Main work of the last few days consisted of trying to make sense of my design process and making an attempt of synthesis.

From studies of behaviour


Focus: the object

In the first part of the year I was much interested in the theme of companionship with robots and electronic devices at large. In her book Alone Together, Sherry Turkle writes about it like follows:

We are lonely but fearful of intimacy. Digital connection and sociable robots may offer the illusion of companionship without the demand of friendship". I feel something in my stomach when I am not next to my phone and it seems like I am missing a limb when I am in a pub without my laptop stored in my backpack on my back. I would like to investigate further the very meaning of companionship with technological devices and the digital world in general.

In all that, it seemed to me that the focus of my research should have been put on the behaviour of these electronic companions, as a sine qua non condition for engagement with humans. From that, I build four prototypes where I tried to subvert a paradigm. Instead of building "a robot that caresses you" where clearly the focus is about the interaction with it, I named them with their particular associated attitude.
.1 A caring robot: a study on pain and shared empathy;
.2 An intimate robot: a study on intimacy;
.3 A silly robot: a study on unpredictable and irreverent behaviour;
.4 A tamed robot: a study on obedience and servile attitude;



Moving further from the independent interaction studies towards the possible applications with a UR-10 robotic arm, I got inspired by puppetry. I built a reproduction of a robotic arm that could let me explore:
-movement through a set of poses of the robotic arm;
-interactivity with a set of end effectors.

For some unknown reason I was not satisfied with these explorations, as I thought that behaviour alone was weak. I felt the need of designing controllers that were more direct and precise, therefore switching the focus of the project from the objects to the user.

To studies of direct control


Focus: the user

The second part of my research involved designing and testing a set of direct controllers. Inspired by science-fiction characters, the interface allows for a direct manipulation of a UR10 robotic arm. Looking back at the initial theme of companionship, it can be argued that here it is put equal to human mastery over technology.

A difficult match

What is being difficult at this moment is to reconceliate the two sides of the project. On the one hand, can companionship only be fueled by nature-like behaviour, sacrificing a bit of human control for the sake of expressivity? On the other hand, can we call companionship a form of direct control?
It is quite clear to me that the second part of my project led me to a bit of a dreadlock, while the first part was far more stimulating.

Conclusions

I am most likely taking a step back from the controller/interface section. I am not even sure that the UR10 is really the way to go. I wouldnt have used blue foam, but I wish Nicole's project was mine.
