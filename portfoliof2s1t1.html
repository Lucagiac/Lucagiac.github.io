About Us

a story on human-robot dialogues, with a focus on the design process during which I was quite confident with my skills

written in the summer of 2017, settled in London, UK
“We are lonely but fearful of intimacy. Digital connection and sociable robots may offer the illusion of companionship without the demand of friendship” Sherry Turkle_

Robots are the most sophisticated breed of tools that human can make use of. They are both a cognitive and a physical aid that in some aspects can outperform our skills. What becomes then the very deep meaning of companionship with technological devices and the digital world in general? About us is a human-robot ritual of a typical day routine.

3 minutes read

how it started

During my studies at the Bartlett School of Architecture, I particularly focused on the role of devices ecologies that take active part in our every day lives. We are sourrounded by technological devices and digital networks, yet we mostly feel alone in our digital-physical routines.

Is there a way to give technological devices a soul? Is there a need for such a thing? Would that make us feel better? With these never answered question I turned to theatre and puppetry in order to better comprehend the topic of agency.

Soon it seemed to me that the focus of my research should have been put on the behaviour of these electronic companions, as a sine qua non condition for engagement with humans. From there, I built a set of prototypes where I tried to replicate human behaviours, with the use of puppetry.

The studies involved designing and programming small robots showing pain, shared empathy, a silly attitude, obedience and more.

the aesthetics of robots

The last part of my research investigated the role of aesthetics and material properties in designing human-robot systems. Going beyond paradigms of antromorphism, how does the aesthetics of robots inspire trust in its users?

The following sketch shows a silly puppetry test - using a fake robot with familiar utensils: a spoon, a tissue and a glass.

How to bring it to the next level? I managed to get my hands on a UR-10 robotic arm - a perfect body for a set of utensils (or end-effectors).

So I started to craft different tools, choosing a language that was against the usual design choices in consumer electronics and robotics. I used wood and warm color materials.

final result

In the end, I pointed out a single ritual that was fascinating to me if explored on a human-robot level: going on a date, an intimate moment. Four tools were needed: a brush, a spoon, a tissue and eventually a cigarette holder.

The robotic arm UR-10 was equipped with magnets to "replace" the end effectors during the ritual. It could then become a continuous discourse, in which the robot became a conversational piece.

About us is definitively a speculative project, where I let myself free to explore and pursue bold ideas. Theatre blends with design and with robotics in a cross-disciplinary discourse. Many have associated the result with Chaplins' Modern Times scene of the corn eating machine, which I take as a true compliment.

That's how I graduated from the Bartlett School of Architecture with a provokative design fiction.
