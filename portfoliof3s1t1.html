Paint by Drone

a story on machines generating art, with a focus on the design process during which I was quite confident with my skills

written in the summer of 2019, settled in Turin, Italy
“The city is an open canvas, where people can inscribe their stories in many ways. Such processes have always been happening.” Carlo Ratti_

"Paint by Drone" is the world’s first crowdsourced graffiti, designed by thousands of people on a collaborative web interface and painted by a swarm of drones in the city of Torino, Italy. The final vertical graffiti was created by four drones flying simultaneously over two consecutive days. Each drone carried a tank of spray paint and sketched the individual designs on the canvas. ​

4 minutes read

How can we give a large crowd the chance to collaborate on producing an artwork? And then, how can we make a wise use of technology in order to reproduce such a drawing onto an urban landscape? These very questions were at the basis of the concept - as show in the schematic below.

from a collaborative drawing...

In order to collect contribution from a vast audience, we launched for two months a web-app in which users from all over the world could draw using few rules. In the first place, the interface was very basic: you could draw a line with fingers or mouse track and you could swap between four different colours.

We based the app on a true life metaphor: digital canvas size was respecting the real dimensions of 12x15 meters, hence each user could draw with a digital spray can for about 10 meters only. So we asked the audience to respond to a simple question: the city is an open canvas - what is the city for you?
Needless to say, first results were a bit cahotic.

As an invisible hand over the crowdsourcing process, we acted as a curator, using a backend app which allowed us to rate and evaluate each contribution before it became public. That helped avoiding shameful content and gave us designers a chance to orchestrate the final output.

And below the final result as it came to be: few icons of the city of Turin - including the outline of the Mole Antonelliana - and some more random content.

... to a drone painter

The drone infrastructure looked relatively simple: we took the final drawing, broke it into segments that a drone could reproduce and we created a palimpsest with "flight sessions" - so to give drones time to recharge battery and replace the spray can.

Each drone was equipped with a 300ml spray can with was operated by a sort of a electro-mechanical handle. Besides that, each drone also carried a powerful computing unit for position tracing.

In order to trace the position of the drone in near real-time, the canvas was equipped with 9 antennas and each drone carried a signal relay. The tech system, developed by Links Foundation, is called UWB (ultra wide-band).

And so from the lab we moved to the site to prepare for a month full of testing. It was a warm Italian summer...

final graffiti

Parco Aurelio Peccei is a former IVECO-FIAT factory, located in a multi-cultural neighbourhood on the outskirt of Turin. Choosing to showcase technology and innovation at the perfiphery of the city - hence avoiding Turin's majestic plazas - was surely a political choice.

From a technical perspective some lines are quite messy, as wind turbolences obviously affected the final result. Still isn't it part of the expressive qualities of the brush of the painter - in this case the drone?

That's how we managed to take a crowdsourced painting and drawing it on a 200 square meter facade.
